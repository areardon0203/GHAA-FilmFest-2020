Who We Are - Julian Cucuta

"Who We Are" is a short film inspired by the works of my favorite Instagram photographer, Damon Baker. The grittiness of the black and white filter and what it induces on the models adds an element that never fails to mesmerize me. It gives me visions of twisted, dark royalty which is what I attempted to put together here. Of course, none of it would've mattered without the remarks of the Queen herself.

The Lighthouse - Shaun Mitchell

Inspired by the track "Afraid of Time" from Hans Zimmer's score of the film INTERSTELLAR, I was on a run by the beach when I saw this lighthouse. A lighthouse is a symbol of safety and hope. So I started thinking, how can I translate that into something that tells a story of hope, even when times are bleak as they are. No matter how far the distance is to something, in this case a physical lighthouse, there is always hope. Just like now: while there may not be a vaccine for the virus yet, there is still hope that one day it will come. One day, we'll reach The Lighthouse.



Coppelia Epilogues: After Everyone Has Gone - Vanessa Butler & Andrew Reardon

Based on my experience through dance, the deaf culture in my family, and studying what past acting practitioners have developed to address the “talking-head” phenomenon, I strive to create work that asks: how little information does an audience need to understand a narrative? What kind of information is essential to that understanding? - Vanessa Butler

Long Distances, Short Conversations - Cheyenne Libert

My film is a timelapse, a speedpaint, of my piece Long Distances, Short Conversations.

The materials I used are primarily watercolor paints/pencils, and color pencils. The piece as a whole is based on the theme provided, ""distance and time"". The scene depicts something akin to a temporary long-distance relationship awaiting the time they can reunite digital messages and calls being their only means of communication, an afterthought being how this could be connected to what many are going through right now. Perhaps quarantine may be what's restricting them from visiting one another, a friend who went on a trip and got stuck when everything shut down and is missing home, a family member that lives far away and can't visit each other, etc. Due to there being so many ways this could be related to, I took creative liberty and decided not to put characters in the image as I had originally planned, to keep the idea ambiguous and simplify the overall style of the piece. As for the piece itself, I wanted to challenge myself a bit and use pallets for each half of the image, using two completely different parts of the color spectrum to depict the opposite times of day which translates to just how far the distance is between them. On the left, I went with a pallet I am much more familiar with, using shades of blues and purples to represent the soft sleepy atmosphere of nightfall, the vibrant pink and yellow outside the window are the only exception to the pallet to replicate the colors of a sunset sky. The right side was the hard part, as I used reds yellows and oranges to create an early morning atmosphere that felt laidback, yet full of energy to start the day, outside the window mimicking the colors of an easy-going sunrise sky with a vibrant ombre of yellow, white, and light blue. "

Behind Closed Doors

Behind Closed Doors is a piece that explores interrupted routines during this unprecedented time. It begins with familiar routines that then become irrelevant in the isolation of your own room. In the end, each person has the choice to leave or stay in the confines of their safe space.
